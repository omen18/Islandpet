# Illustrator Brief: IslandPet — Pet Sprite Set

**Use this document verbatim** when DMing illustrators on Cara, X/Twitter,
Instagram, Behance, or in cold emails. Copy-paste it. Don't dilute it.

---

## What I'm hiring you for

I'm building **IslandPet**, an iOS focus app launching on TestFlight in
[N] weeks. I need an illustrator to design a single virtual pet across
4 evolution stages — egg, baby, teen, adult — that will appear in the
main app, on the lock screen, and inside Apple's Dynamic Island.

This is the visual identity of the entire product. The pet *is* the brand.

## Scope (v1 commission)

**One species, four evolution stages, six expressions per stage.**

Think of it like commissioning a single character with full expression
turnaround for animation reference.

### Asset list
- 4 stages × 6 expressions = **24 illustrations**
- Expressions: idle, focusing, happy, sleepy, sad, celebrating
- Plus: **2 transition frames between each adjacent pair** (egg→baby,
  baby→teen, teen→adult) for evolution cinematic — 6 extra frames
- **Total: 30 illustrations**

### Format
- **PNG with transparency**, 1024×1024px master, plus 512 and 256 exports
- Color profile: sRGB
- The pet should read at **22×22 pixels** (Dynamic Island compact size).
  Test this at the design stage — silhouette must be recognizable at
  thumbnail size. This is the hardest constraint.

### Style direction

I want the pet to feel:
- **soft, friendly, plush** — children's-storybook texture, not anime,
  not flat vector
- **slightly over-rendered** — subtle gradient + ambient occlusion under
  the body, like a real plush in soft light
- **rounded geometry** — no hard corners, no spiky shapes; everything
  squashable
- **iOS-native feel** — sit naturally next to Apple's SF Symbols and the
  Dynamic Island; not Pokémon, not cartoon-network

References (please look at these):
- Halide app icon (warmth + softness)
- Threes! game (rounded, soft, premium)
- Finch app pet (warmth, but cuter than Finch)
- Procreate brushes app icon (sophistication)

References to **avoid**:
- Pokémon-style outlines (too kid-coded)
- Flat-vector mascot illustration (too SaaS-coded)
- Anime / chibi style (alienates the >25 demographic)

### Color palette (locked)

The pet's primary body color must be **species-specific**, but for v1 we're
shipping one species: **FlameSprite**.

- Primary: `#FF7A45` (warm orange)
- Secondary: `#FFB36B` (soft amber for highlights)
- Accent: `#FFE4D1` (cream for belly / inner ear / cheek lights)
- Eye: `#1A1A1A` (warm black, never pure black)

Use these as the base; you have freedom on shading. Don't introduce new
hues unless they're for accessories (a graduation cap, a party hat).

### Stage notes

- **Egg**: an actual egg, with subtle internal-light shimmer suggesting
  something alive inside. Cracks visible only on the "evolving" transition
  frame.
- **Baby**: maximum cuteness. Big head:body ratio (3:5). Big eyes. Tiny
  limbs. Should make people audibly say "oh."
- **Teen**: lankier, head:body shifts to 1:2. A little awkward. Still
  cute, but more individual.
- **Adult**: confident, balanced. Head:body 1:3. Has *one* signature
  detail the previous stages didn't (small flame on tail, or markings
  on cheek — your call). This signature is what users will remember.

## What I will not accept

- Asset packs / templated mascots — this character must be original.
- AI-generated art presented as hand-drawn. (I'll ask. Be honest.)
- Style that doesn't survive the 22px shrink test.
- Late delivery without communication.

## Timeline & process

- **Week 1**: 3 thumbnail sketches of the egg + baby (single expression each).
  I pick a direction. I pay 25% kill-fee here if we don't proceed.
- **Week 2**: Refined egg + baby across all 6 expressions. I review.
  Up to 1 round of revisions.
- **Week 3**: Teen + adult across all 6 expressions, plus transition frames.
- **Week 4**: Final delivery + minor polish.

I sign off in writing at each weekly checkpoint. No surprises.

## Deliverables

- 30 PNGs (sized as above)
- 1 layered Procreate / Photoshop / Affinity file per stage (so I can
  request small edits without re-commissioning)
- Permission to use the work commercially in IslandPet, on the App Store,
  in marketing, and in press. **Non-exclusive license is fine** —
  you can keep it in your portfolio, post it, sell prints. I don't need
  the copyright; I need the license.

## Budget

I'm offering **$[X]** flat, paid 25% on commission, 25% on egg+baby
delivery, 50% on full delivery.

Reasonable ranges as of 2026:
- Junior illustrator (1–3 yr exp): $400–800

you have signal that the product works.

If you're outside this range, please tell me — I might be flexible if
your portfolio is exceptional.

# Where to find illustrators (ranked)

1. **Cara** (cara.app) — the post-Twitter portfolio site for illustrators
   leaving Adobe / Behance. High signal, illustrators care about quality.
2. **Behance** with filters: Illustration > Character Design > 2024–2026.
   Sort by "Most Appreciated."
3. **X/Twitter** — search `"open for commissions" character design`.
   DMs work. Be specific in the DM, not vague.
4. **Are.na** — search "character design" channels. Niche but high quality.
5. **Reddit r/HungryArtists** and r/artcommissions — high volume,
   variable quality. Use the brief as a filter.

**Avoid** Fiverr (race-to-the-bottom), Upwork (wrong audience),
99designs (asset-flip dropshippers), DeviantArt comments
(usually amateurs).

# Red flags when reviewing portfolios

- Every piece has the same face / pose → templating, not character work
- Heavy filter use covering technique gaps
- No sketches or process work shown → can't iterate
- "Master of every style" → master of none
- Recent posts are AI-flagged → not what you're paying for

# Green flags

- A few characters fully turned around (front/side/back/expressions)
- Visible iteration: thumbnails → refined → final
- A coherent personal style across the body of work
- One or two pieces in your reference range, even if not exact
